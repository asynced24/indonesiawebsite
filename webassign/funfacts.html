Fun Facts!

Here are 3 birds from Ontario that I find interesting:


1. Long Point, Port Rowan, Ontario:

A Red-bellied Woodpecker can stick out its tongue nearly 2 inches past the end of its beak.
Red-bellied Woodpeckers have been known to take over the nests of other birds, including the much smaller (and endangered) Red-cockaded Woodpecker.

  

2. Snowy Owl:

Bristles on the Snowy Owl's beaks help them sense nearby objects. The beak that is nearly covered by facial feathers is hooked and used for gripping prey and tearing flesh.
Needing insulation from Arctic temperatures, snowy owls have a lot of feathers. This makes them one of the heaviest owl species in North America.

3. American Robin :

The American robin uses pieces of straw, twigs, and dry leaves to build cup-shaped nests, and add moss, mud, and sometimes feathers to mend and line the interiors.
The American robin is active mostly during the day and assembles in large flocks at night.
It is among the earliest birds to sing at dawn, and its song consists of several discrete units that are repeated.
